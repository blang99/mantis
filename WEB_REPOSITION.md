# MANTIS — Web Repositioning Brief (0.5.1)

*Storytelling + marketing analysis for the landing page, aligned to the Ambient Partner
reposition. Companion to [AMBIENT_VISION.md](AMBIENT_VISION.md). Written 2026-06-13.*

---

## 1. The strategic shift in one line

**Old story (≤0.5.0):** "The AI that *builds* your Grasshopper definition." A better vending
machine — you ask, it generates native nodes. *Watch it think. Watch it build.*

**New story (0.5.1):** "The design partner that's *in the room*." A presence that watches your
canvas silently and only speaks when it has caught a **real, verified** failure and you're stuck —
then fixes it in one click, on your actual graph.

The build engine doesn't go away. It gets **demoted from headline to proof-point**: evidence the
partner is competent, not the reason to install.

## 2. Why reposition at all (the marketing case)

- **The generator category is commoditized.** Raven (~3,600 users, $49/mo), Ant, Kea, GHPT, Reer —
  all ship "text→native GH." "We generate nodes too" is table stakes, not a wedge. Competing there
  is a feature race we don't win on distribution.
- **"Always-on partner" is an *uncontested* category — and a structurally defensible one.** Every
  competitor runs on **metered cloud AI**: watching your canvas all day would cost them money on
  every glance, so their unit economics *forbid* it. Mantis runs on the user's own key or a local
  model, where watching costs ~nothing. **This is the one product shape the incumbents are
  economically barred from copying** — they can clone a feature in a weekend; they can't clone their
  way out of their own pricing.
- **The pain we now sell to is bigger and unowned.** The expensive moment in GH isn't asking a
  question — it's the 20 minutes a definition is silently broken (green, solves, produces *nothing*)
  and nobody's watching. No tool addresses this because no tool is *present*.
- **The data moat only exists if we watch.** Because Mantis is the only one watching, it alone sees
  *where people get stuck* (not what they ask). Opt-in, anonymized, that becomes a living
  friction-map competitors start at zero.

## 3. The reframed messaging spine

| Layer | Old | New (0.5.1) |
|-------|-----|-------------|
| Category | AI script generator for GH | The always-on design partner in Grasshopper |
| Headline | Watch it think. Watch it build. | **It watches. It almost never speaks.** |
| Hero proof | 3D twisting tower you configure | **The orb** — breathing green → amber → one-click verified fix |
| Core verb | *Generate* | *Notice · Verify · Fix* |
| The build engine | The whole product | A supporting chapter: "and yes — it still builds" |
| Trust frame | "No black boxes" | **Not Clippy:** silent by default, right when it speaks, reversible, backs off |
| Sovereignty | Your key, your machine | Same — now also the *enabler* of always-on (the moat, not just a feature) |

### Voice
Calm, precise, confident-quiet — the product's personality *is* the brand. Short declaratives.
No hype verbs. Let the one perfect catch ("your panel grid went empty when you rewired Divide —
fix it?") carry the weight. The page should feel like the orb: mostly silent, exact when it speaks.

## 4. Page architecture — Scroll-Triggered Storytelling

Mapped from the AMBIENT_VISION "day in the life." Each section is a beat of the same story, building
to the install CTA.

1. **Hero — the hook.** The orb (prime slot, where the tower was). *It watches. It almost never
   speaks.* Sub = the presence promise. CTAs: Install in Rhino · See how it works. Eyebrow:
   `Always on · Silent by default · Runs on your machine`.
2. **The problem.** "Most of the pain isn't the question. It's the 20 minutes before it." The silent
   empty-output failure; nobody watching.
3. **The shift / insight.** "Not a box you talk to. A partner in the room." The good-assistant analogy.
4. **A day with the orb (the journey).** The 3-state walkthrough: calm green → quiet amber on a
   silent break → hover → the fix card → the receipt ("Fixed — now produces 96 panels").
5. **And yes, it still builds (the proof-point).** The legacy engine as a *supporting* chapter —
   the animated chat demo: type a prompt → it thinks → the twisted tower appears. "When you *do*
   want it to build, it's an expert that wires the real canvas."
6. **Why only Mantis can do this (the moat).** Metered cloud can't afford to watch; local/BYO-key
   can. The friction-map flywheel.
7. **Not Clippy (trust).** The four principles: silent by default · verified before spoken · always
   reversible · earns every interruption.
8. **Sovereignty.** Runs on your machine; your key or fully local; everything editable.
9. **Climax CTA.** Install in two minutes. `_PackageManager → "MANTIS"`.

## 5. What carries over (don't rebuild the craft)
Keep the visual system intact — it's already right: dark OLED (`--bg #050a07`), Mantis green
(`#5CDB7A`) with amber (`#e8b84d`) reserved for *verify/caution*, Orbitron/Space Grotesk/JetBrains
Mono, node-graph motifs, the node-field canvas, the preloader. The reposition is **narrative +
two new interactive demos (orb, chat-build)**, not a restyle.

## 6. Success criteria for the page
- A first-time visitor can state, in one sentence, *what's different about Mantis* ("it watches and
  fixes silent breaks") — not "another AI that builds GH graphs."
- The amber/fix-card moment is the thing they remember.
- The build engine reads as *reassuring competence*, not the pitch.
- Honest: nothing on the page claims proactive magic we haven't verified (M4 still pending) — the
  orb demo is illustrative; copy stays truthful to what ships.
