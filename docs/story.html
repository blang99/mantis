Story

The assistant that's
actually in the room.

Every AI for Grasshopper is a box you stop and talk to. MANTIS was built on a different bet: that the real help isn't a smarter vending machine — it's a presence that watches the work with you and catches the silent break before it costs you the afternoon.

The frustration

The 20 minutes nobody sees.

The expensive moment in Grasshopper isn't asking a question. It's the twenty minutes a definition is quietly broken — green, solving, producing nothing — because a wire upstream is feeding the wrong data. Grasshopper doesn't warn you. The AI in the panel doesn't know, because you didn't ask. Nobody is watching the work with you.

The wrong fix

A smarter vending machine is still a vending machine.

Every tool answered by being a better box: you stop, turn to a panel, type, wait, read, turn back. But a good human assistant doesn't wait to be asked. They're in the room — they glance over and say "your loft is empty because that curve isn't closed." Quiet most of the time; right when they speak.

The bet

Be present, not summoned.

So MANTIS watches your canvas the whole time, verifies what it sees against the live solve, and only speaks when it's caught something real and you're stuck — then fixes it on your actual graph in one undo. It runs on your machine, on your key or fully local. The reason no metered-cloud tool can follow: watching all day is free for you, and billed for them.

What we won't compromise

Four principles.

01

Silent by default

Presence is a quiet light, not a chat that talks first. It speaks only when it caught something real and you're genuinely stuck. Dismiss it once and it backs off.

02

Verified before spoken

It only asserts what it machine-checked against your live solve. "This is empty and here's why" is a fact, not a hunch. That's how it earns the right to interrupt.

03

Your machine, your model

Bring your own key or run fully local. Your geometry never leaves; only opt-in, anonymous, corroborated lessons do. Every fix is one undo.

04

Open source

MIT licensed, on GitHub. Read it, fork it, audit exactly what it sends. Trust is earned by being inspectable.
