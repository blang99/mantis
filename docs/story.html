Story

Parametric design deserves
an honest assistant.

Most "AI for CAD" hides the work — a cloud call, a mesh you can't open, a subscription meter running. MANTIS was built on the opposite bet: that designers want to see the logic and own the file.

The frustration

The wall every Grasshopper user hits.

You know what you want — a diagrid that responds to an attractor, a facade that panelizes cleanly, a stair with adjustable rise. But getting there means remembering which thirty components to drag out and how to wire them. The idea is instant; the assembly is not.

The wrong fix

Generated scripts aren't the answer.

The first wave of AI-CAD tools answered with opaque C# blocks or cloud-rendered meshes. They produce a result — but not one you can read, tune, or learn from. The moment you want to change a parameter, you're stuck, because there's no graph to change.

The bet

Build the real thing, on the real canvas.

MANTIS writes the actual native Grasshopper definition — every component placed, wired, grouped and labelled — and streams its reasoning so you learn the move as it happens. It runs on your machine, with your model. When it's done, you have a file that's entirely yours.

What we won't compromise

Four principles.

01

Native or nothing

If the output isn't a real, editable Grasshopper graph, it isn't MANTIS. No black boxes, no proprietary geometry you can't extend.

02

Show the reasoning

Every build streams a plan. You should finish a session knowing more about parametric design than you started with.

03

Your machine, your model

Bring your own key or run fully local. We never put ourselves between you and your work, and never meter it.

04

Open source

MIT licensed, on GitHub. Read it, fork it, audit exactly what it sends. Trust is earned by being inspectable.
