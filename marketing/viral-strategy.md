# MANTIS — Viral Marketing Strategy

## Core Positioning

**One-liner:** "Talk to Grasshopper. Build anything."

**Elevator pitch:** MANTIS is a free, open-source Grasshopper plugin that turns natural language into native parametric components — live on your canvas. No Python, no code, no subscriptions. Just describe what you want.

**Why it goes viral:** The architecture/design community is visual and shares work constantly. A 15-second screen recording of someone typing "create a parametric facade with hexagonal panels" and watching components materialize on the canvas is inherently shareable. It looks like magic.

---

## Viral Content Playbook

### 1. The "Magic Moment" Video (Primary Viral Asset)

**Format:** 30-60 second screen recording, no voiceover, captions only
**Platform:** Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Instagram Reels

**Script:**
```
[Caption] "I just told Grasshopper what to build."

[Screen: Empty Grasshopper canvas]
[Type in MANTIS panel: "Create a parametric tower with circular floor plates that taper from 20m at base to 8m at top, 15 floors"]

[Watch components appear one by one — sliders, circles, move, loft]
[Camera zooms to Rhino viewport — tower appears]

[Caption] "Every component is real. Fully editable. No code."

[Show: editing a slider, tower updates live]

[Caption] "MANTIS — free and open source"
[Logo + download link]
```

**Why it works:** Architects are trained to notice when something removes pain. Grasshopper is notoriously hard to learn. Seeing natural language → instant visual result triggers "I need this" immediately.

---

### 2. The "Before/After" Split-Screen

**Format:** Side-by-side comparison image or 15s video
**Platform:** Instagram, Pinterest, Twitter/X

**Left side:** "Building a Voronoi facade the normal way"
- Screenshot of 30+ tangled components
- Timer: "45 minutes"

**Right side:** "Building it with MANTIS"
- Clean, auto-laid-out components
- Timer: "12 seconds"
- Chat panel showing the natural language prompt

**Caption:** "Same result. One took 45 minutes. One took a sentence."

---

### 3. The "Architecture Student Challenge"

**Format:** Challenge/trend format
**Platform:** TikTok, Instagram Reels

**Concept:** "Can AI build your thesis project in 30 seconds?"

1. Student describes their thesis concept in one sentence
2. MANTIS generates the parametric script
3. Compare with their actual Grasshopper definition

**Why it works:** Students share everything. The challenge format encourages participation. The comparison is always dramatic because student definitions are messy and MANTIS output is clean.

**Hashtags:** #GrasshopperChallenge #AIArchitecture #ParametricDesign #MantisGH

---

### 4. The "Explain This" Series (Educational Content)

**Format:** 60s educational videos
**Platform:** YouTube Shorts, TikTok, LinkedIn

**Concept:** Open a complex Grasshopper definition, click "Explain" mode, click on a component chain → MANTIS explains in plain English what it does.

**Series titles:**
- "What does THIS component chain even do?"
- "My professor's Grasshopper definition, explained by AI"
- "Decoding 200 components in 10 seconds"

**Why it works:** Grasshopper definitions are famously incomprehensible. The explain feature has massive educational appeal. Teachers share tools that help students.

---

### 5. The "Free vs Paid" Narrative

**Format:** Text post / thread
**Platform:** Twitter/X, Reddit, Grasshopper forums

**Hook:** "I built an AI Grasshopper plugin with 4 AI providers — including ones that are completely free."

**Thread:**
1. "Most AI tools for architects require a $20/month subscription. MANTIS works with free models out of the box."
2. "Gemini 2.0 Flash — free API, fast, handles most scripts perfectly"
3. "Ollama — runs entirely on your laptop. No internet. No API key. Zero cost."
4. "Or use Claude/OpenRouter for complex scripts. Your choice, always."
5. "Oh, and it's open source. Fork it, modify it, contribute."

**Why it works:** The architecture community is price-sensitive (especially students). Free + open source is a powerful differentiation against Raven (paid, closed-source).

## Viral Amplifiers

### 1. Open Source Advantage
GitHub stars are social proof. Encourage starring with:
- "If MANTIS saved you time, give us a star ⭐"
- Star count badge on landing page
- Community contributions → featured on social

### 2. User-Generated Content
- Create a **#BuiltWithMantis** hashtag
- Monthly showcase of best community builds
- Repost/amplify user content

### 3. Integration with Existing Community
- Submit to awesome-grasshopper lists
- Write guest posts for computational design blogs
- Present at local Rhino user groups

### 4. Academic Network Effect
- One professor adopts → entire class downloads
- Workshop at parametric design conferences (SmartGeometry, ACADIA)
- Free licenses for educational institutions (it's already free, but brand the message)
